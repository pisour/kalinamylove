The real part

Hey love, I’ve been sitting with this.

I’m sorry for making you feel like you had to repeat yourself. I know I sometimes understand my own thoughts by saying them out loud, but I can see how that felt on your side after we had already talked about it. It could feel like I wasn’t listening closely, or like I was asking you to carry the same explanation again.

That would be frustrating. I get that now, and I’m sorry.

There is another part I need to own too: therapy. I told you I would take that seriously, and I haven’t followed through in the clear, consistent way I said I would. I don’t want to hide behind the fact that I have been trying in other ways. Meditation, healthier routines, work, and being more present have helped me, but they do not replace the promise I made to you.

I don’t want you to feel like my growth only happens when it is convenient, or that I make a promise and quietly swap it for something easier. You deserve honesty from me, not a vague cloud around something I said mattered.

I love you. I don’t want us stuck in silence or trapped in the same argument with different details. I’m not saying I meant to hurt you, and I’m not saying your feelings are wrong. I’m saying I understand how it landed. I’m sorry for my part in it. I want to do better in a way you can actually feel.

Next time, when I’m thinking out loud, I’ll say that clearly instead of letting it sound like I’m asking you to repeat yourself. And with therapy, I’ll be honest about where I’m at and what I’m doing next, so the promise does not just sit there between us.
